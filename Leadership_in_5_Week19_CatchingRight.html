5 : 1

Research shows high-performing teams experience 5 positive interactions for every 1 corrective one. Most leaders are closer to 1:1 — or worse.

What · So What · Now What
What

Active recognition

Catching people doing it right isn't passive — it's a deliberate habit. It means training your attention to notice what's going well, and then saying it out loud with enough specificity that it actually lands.

So What

Generic praise is invisible

"Great job today" is background noise. Specific recognition is unforgettable. When people know exactly what they did well, they do it again — and they remember who noticed.

Now What

Use the formula below

Three parts: what you saw, why it mattered, what it says about them. Takes 20 seconds. Lands for days.

The Recognition Formula

I noticed + the specific behavior + the impact

I noticed

"I noticed how you handled that gate change under pressure —"

Specific

"you kept the team calm, communicated clearly, and didn't miss a beat —"

Impact

"that's exactly the kind of leadership that makes this team better."

Skip these

  • "Good job today" — too vague to mean anything
  • Saving it for the next review — recognition has a short shelf life
  • Group praise when individual recognition is what's earned
✓ Self-Check — How Would You Handle This?

What's your move?

A team member just handled a difficult passenger situation really well — stayed calm, found a solution, and kept everyone moving. You saw the whole thing. What do you do?
The Answer
C — Specific, immediate, direct

Option A misses the moment entirely — "just doing their job" is how great people start to feel invisible. Option B puts it in a drawer where it loses all its power. Option D is well-meaning but dilutes the individual recognition they earned. Option C is the move: specific behavior, real impact, delivered now. Twenty seconds of that is worth more than a quarterly review full of "keeps performing well."
