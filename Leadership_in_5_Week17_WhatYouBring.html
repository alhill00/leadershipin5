Leadership in 5

What You Bring
Into the Room

Executive presence starts before you say a word.
Champion Others | Inspiring & Motivating

You've had that experience — walking into a meeting and immediately sensing someone's energy. Before they spoke, you knew the tone. You do the same thing to your team, every single day. The question is whether you're choosing what you bring, or just carrying whatever happened last.

What · So What · Now What
What

Intentional Presence

Executive presence isn't about how you look or how you speak — it's the intentional choice to bring a specific energy into each interaction. Leaders who practice this ask themselves one question before walking into any conversation: "What do I want this person to feel when I leave?"

So What

Your mood is contagious

Research shows that a leader's emotional state spreads to the team within minutes. If you walk in distracted, rushed, or carrying residue from your last meeting — your team mirrors it. Your presence doesn't just set the tone for the conversation. It sets the ceiling for what's possible in it.

Now What

The 60-Second Reset

Before your next 3 important conversations:

1
Pause for 60 seconds before walking in
2

3
Walk in with that intention as your North Star

What's your move?

You just got off a stressful ops call with unexpected bad news. Your 1-on-1 with a high-potential team member starts in 4 minutes. What do you do?
The Answer
C — The 60-Second Reset

Option A is optimistic — emotional state bleeds through even when we believe it won't. Option B gives up too easily; most conversations recover with a quick reset, not a cancellation. Option D can occasionally work for authenticity, but making your team absorb your stress isn't presence — it's burden-shifting. Option C is the executive presence move: you're not suppressing your emotion, you're choosing what to do with it before it lands on someone else.
